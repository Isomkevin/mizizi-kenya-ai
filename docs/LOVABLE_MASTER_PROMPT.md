# The Mission

Mizizi exists because agricultural finance does not fail due to lack of money.

It fails because risk is difficult to:

- See
- Verify
- Explain

Millions of smallholder farmers already generate trustworthy signals every day.

These include:

- Cooperative repayment history
- Mobile money activity
- Input purchase behaviour
- Climate conditions
- Community relationships
- Peer repayment behaviour
- Farm productivity

Unfortunately these signals exist inside disconnected systems.

As a result,

Loan officers cannot see the complete picture.

Insurance companies cannot accurately verify farm-level risk.

Farmers cannot understand lending decisions.

Mizizi connects these disconnected pieces into one explainable intelligence layer.

# Why Graph Intelligence Changes Everything

Traditional relational databases answer questions like:

- What happened?
- Who owns this record?
- What is the repayment amount?

They struggle to answer relationship-based questions such as:

- Which farmers are connected to trusted repayment communities?
- Which cooperatives consistently produce low-risk borrowers?
- How does climate risk affect this applicant relative to neighboring farms?
- Which peer relationships increase lending confidence?
- Which connected events contributed to today's decision?

Mizizi is built on Graph Intelligence because agricultural finance is fundamentally a relationship problem.

Neo4j is not used as a visualization layer.

It is the system of intelligence that powers every decision.

Every risk score must be traceable through graph relationships.

Every explanation must be grounded in graph facts.

Every visualization must reinforce this connected worldview.

---

# Explainable AI

One of Mizizi's biggest differentiators is that AI never replaces the loan officer.

The system follows one principle:

> AI explains.
> Humans decide.

The workflow should always be:

Graph Data
↓

Graph Algorithms
↓

Risk Factors
↓

Large Language Model
↓

Human-readable Explanation
↓

Loan Officer Decision

The language model must never invent reasons.

It only translates graph-derived facts into clear, concise explanations.

Two explanation formats should always be generated:

## Loan Officer Explanation

A comprehensive explanation showing:

- contributing relationships
- confidence score
- graph paths
- supporting evidence
- climate influences
- cooperative behaviour
- repayment behaviour
- risk rationale

---

## Farmer Explanation

A short explanation suitable for:

- SMS
- USSD
- WhatsApp

Maximum length:

160–320 characters.

Example:

"You have consistently repaid cooperative loans, which improved your application. Current drought conditions increased your risk. Maintaining repayment and using certified inputs next season may improve future eligibility."

The tone should always be respectful, empowering, and actionable.

Never expose proprietary scoring formulas.
